# Evidence — Foundations (residual stream, QK/OV circuits, linear representation hypothesis)

Status: 3/3 questions answered. Compiled 2026-07-01.

## Q1: The residual-stream view of a transformer (Elhage et al. 2021, "A Mathematical Framework for Transformer Circuits"): what is the residual stream as a communication channel, how do attention heads and MLPs READ from and WRITE to it, and what are "virtual weights"/direct-path terms?

- **Finding:** The residual stream is the running sum of the token/positional embedding plus every subsequent attention-head and MLP-layer output; because every layer's contribution is *added* to it rather than replacing it, the stream functions as a shared, high-bandwidth communication channel that every component (embedding, each attention head, each MLP neuron, the unembedding) both reads from (via a linear projection) and writes to (via a linear map back into the stream). Because all reads/writes are linear and the connections between any two components are purely additive, the transformer's computational graph is "fully connected" — any earlier component's output can influence any later component — and the effective end-to-end linear map between two components that never directly interact (e.g., embedding → a single attention head's OV output → unembedding) can be computed by composing their write/read matrices; this composed matrix is called a **virtual weight**, and it is not literally instantiated anywhere in the network's parameters — it is a derived quantity used to analyze the "direct path" interaction.
- **Mechanism / derivation notes:**
  - Write: $x_0 = W_E \cdot \text{tok} (+ \text{pos})$ (embedding writes into the stream).
  - Update rule per layer $l$: $x_l = x_{l-1} + \text{Attn}_l(x_{l-1}) + \text{MLP}_l(x_{l-1})$ — strictly additive, no overwriting.
  - Read: attention head $h$ reads the stream through low-rank projections $W_Q^h, W_K^h, W_V^h \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{model}\times d_{head}}$ (rank $\le d_{head} \ll d_{model}$); MLP neurons read through $W_{in}$.
  - Final read: logits $= W_U \cdot x_L$ (unembedding).
  - **Virtual weights / direct path**: because every step is a linear map (until a nonlinearity like softmax or ReLU intervenes), any two components' matrices can be multiplied together along a path through the stream to get the "virtual" effective weight of that path — e.g. the *direct-path logit* term is $W_U W_E$ (skip everything, bypass all layers); a single head's direct effect on logits is $W_U W_O^h W_V^h W_E$ (its OV circuit's contribution, see Q2). The paper frames analysis of any pair of components as "multiply the writing weights of one by the reading weights of the other" to get a crude proxy for their interaction, valid on the linear portions of the path.
  - **Bandwidth-bottleneck argument**: the residual stream's dimensionality ($d_{model}$) is far smaller than the sum of all components' internal dimensionalities (e.g. a single MLP layer alone typically has ~4× more neurons than $d_{model}$), so components cannot all get disjoint orthogonal subspaces — they must share/compress the stream, which the paper connects to the model needing to operate in **superposition** (see Q3) and is why individual residual-stream coordinates are not case to interpret directly; the framework instead studies virtual-weight decompositions.
  - **No privileged basis (structural)**: the residual stream by itself has no privileged basis — you could rotate the entire stream by an orthogonal matrix $R$ and simultaneously rotate every writing/reading weight matrix by $R$ (or $R^{-1}$) without changing the function computed, so individual coordinate axes of the raw stream carry no special meaning a priori (contrast with post-nonlinearity bases, e.g. MLP neuron activations after ReLU, which typically *are* privileged — elementwise nonlinearities break the rotational symmetry).
- **Values:** n/a — this Q is structural/definitional; no headline numeric result in the framework paper itself. (One tangential number surfaced in search snippets — "20-60 outlier dimensions out of $d_{model}=1280$" — could not be traced to a specific paper/section in the time budget; flagged as an unverified/possibly-misattributed snippet, do not cite it without re-verification.)

## Q2: QK vs OV circuits — factorization of a head into query-key circuit ($W_Q^\top W_K$) and output-value circuit ($W_O W_V$); what each computes (where-to-attend vs what-to-move); head composition (Q-, K-, V-composition). Matrix forms.

- **Finding:** Each attention head decomposes into two functionally-independent linear circuits: the **QK circuit**, $W_{QK}^h = W_Q^{h\top} W_K^h$, which is a bilinear form over pairs of residual-stream states that determines *which* source token(s) a destination position attends to (the attention pattern), and the **OV circuit**, $W_{OV}^h = W_V^h W_O^h$ (order depends on row/column convention; equivalently $W_O W_V$ acting on a column-vector residual state), which determines *what* gets moved/written into the residual stream once a source token is attended to. The two circuits are independent in the sense that the attention *pattern* (from QK) and the *information moved* (from OV) can be analyzed and manipulated separately. **Head composition** is the mechanism by which a later head's computation can depend on an earlier head's output through the shared residual stream, and comes in three flavors depending on which of the later head's three input matrices reads the virtual output of the earlier head: **Q-composition** (earlier head's OV output feeds the later head's $W_Q$ — changes what the later head's query "searches for"), **K-composition** (feeds $W_K$ — changes what a position "advertises" as a key), and **V-composition** (feeds $W_V$ — changes what content gets copied, without changing the attention pattern itself). K-composition is the mechanism underlying **induction heads**.
- **Mechanism / derivation notes:**
  - Per-head weights: $W_Q^h, W_K^h, W_V^h \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{model}\times d_{head}}$, $W_O^h \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{head}\times d_{model}}$; each factor is individually rank $\le d_{head} \ll d_{model}$, so $W_{QK}^h$ and $W_{OV}^h$ are each rank $\le d_{head}$ — a structural bottleneck.
  - Attention score between destination $i$ and source $j$: $\text{score}(i,j) = x_i W_Q^h (x_j W_K^h)^\top = x_i \left(W_Q^h W_K^{h\top}\right) x_j^\top = x_i\, W_{QK}^h\, x_j^\top$; attention pattern $A_{ij} = \text{softmax}_j\!\left(\text{score}(i,j)/\sqrt{d_{head}}\right)$ — this is the QK circuit's entire job (where-to-attend), and it is bilinear in the *pair* $(x_i, x_j)$, never seeing "content to move."
  - Head's write-back to the stream: $\text{out}_i = \sum_j A_{ij}\, \big(x_j\, W_V^h\big) W_O^h = \sum_j A_{ij}\, x_j\, W_{OV}^h$ — the OV circuit's entire job (what-to-move): a fixed linear map applied to whatever token gets attended, independent of the attention pattern that selected it.
  - **Full end-to-end (virtual-weight) forms**, tracing through embedding/unembedding (bigram-style analysis): the QK circuit's effect on token-pair compatibility is $W_E\, W_{QK}^h\, W_E^\top$ (a token $\times$ token compatibility/bigram table — "does token $a$ attend to token $b$?"), and the OV circuit's effect on output logits is $W_E\, W_{OV}^h\, W_U$ (a token $\times$ token copying table — "if I attend to token $b$, how much does it promote/suppress each vocabulary logit?"). These full-circuit matrices are what the framework calls "virtual weights" applied to a single head in isolation (0-layer / 1-layer analysis).
  - **Composition** (2+ layer effects): because a later head's $W_Q, W_K, W_V$ read the *residual stream*, and the residual stream by layer $\ell{>}1$ already contains an earlier head's OV write, the later head's effective query/key/value is a virtual combination of the raw embedding *and* the earlier head's output. Concretely, if head $A$ (earlier layer) writes $x\,W_{OV}^A$ into the stream, and head $B$ (later layer) forms its key as $\big(x + x\,W_{OV}^A\big) W_K^B$, then the term $x\,W_{OV}^A W_K^B$ is the **K-composition** virtual weight — head $B$'s "key" is now a function of what head $A$ moved, not just the raw token. Analogous terms $W_{OV}^A W_Q^B$ (Q-composition) and $W_{OV}^A W_V^B$ (V-composition) are defined the same way for the other two matrices. Q- and K-composition change the *attention pattern* the later head computes (it can now be a function of the model's intermediate representations, not just literal input tokens); V-composition changes only the *content* moved, not where it's moved from.
  - **Induction heads** (canonical 2-layer example, needs K-composition): a layer-1 "previous-token" head writes, at every position $j$, information about token $j{-}1$ into the stream. A layer-2 induction head K-composes with it: its key vector at position $j$ therefore effectively encodes "the token that followed $j{-}1$'s original position when this pattern last occurred," while its query at the current final position $i$ is (roughly) the current token $x_i$. The QK circuit then matches "find the earlier occurrence of the current token," and the OV circuit copies whatever token immediately followed that earlier occurrence — implementing the algorithm "if $[A][B] \ldots [A]$ appears, predict $[B]$ next," i.e., in-context pattern-completion / a mechanistic basis for a chunk of in-context learning.
- **Values:** Structural only — each head's $W_{QK}$ and $W_{OV}$ are rank $\le d_{head}$ (a design bottleneck, not a fitted number). No specific accuracy/ablation numbers retrieved in this pass (would come from Olsson et al. 2022's induction-head detection metrics, not directly queried this round).

## Q3: The linear representation hypothesis / features-as-directions (Olah et al. 2020, "Zoom In: An Introduction to Circuits," distill.pub; framework papers): what is a "feature," what does "privileged basis" mean, and what is the empirical support and pushback?

- **Finding:** A **feature** is defined as a direction in a layer's activation space — i.e., a specific linear combination of neurons (a unit vector in $\mathbb{R}^{d}$) — that corresponds to a human-interpretable property of the input (e.g., "curve detector," "sentiment," "is_animal"). The **linear representation hypothesis (LRH)** generalizes this into the working assumption that high-level, human-interpretable concepts are encoded as (approximately) linear directions in a model's representation space, so that an activation vector can be approximately decomposed as a sparse sum of such feature directions, and concepts can be read out / edited via linear probes and additive "steering vectors." A **privileged basis** is a basis (coordinate system) in which individual axes are more meaningful than an arbitrary rotation of those axes; the residual stream, by itself, has *no* privileged basis (you can rotate the whole stream, plus all matrices reading/writing it, without changing the function computed — see Q1), but bases immediately following an elementwise nonlinearity (e.g., MLP neuron activations post-ReLU, or an SAE's dictionary basis) typically *are* privileged, because the nonlinearity is applied per-coordinate and thus breaks rotational symmetry — that asymmetry is exactly why the "neuron" basis in an MLP is a more natural place to look for interpretable features than the residual-stream basis itself.
- **Mechanism / derivation notes:**
  - Olah et al. (2020) frame three linked claims underpinning the "circuits" program: (1) Features are the fundamental unit of neural networks and are best understood as directions (not always single neurons — polysemantic neurons respond to multiple unrelated features, so the interpretable unit can be a linear combination across several neurons); (2) Features connect to form Circuits (meaningful algorithms built from feature-detector weights); (3) Analogous features/circuits recur across architectures/tasks ("universality").
  - Elhage et al. (2022), "Toy Models of Superposition," give the formal mechanism for *why* a privileged, feature-aligned basis is not always the neuron basis: when a model has more sparse, rarely-co-occurring features to represent than it has available dimensions, it can pack them as **almost-orthogonal (not exactly orthogonal) directions** — "superposition" — trading a small amount of interference noise for the ability to represent far more features than dimensions; this only works because features are sparse (rarely all active at once) and the network can rely on a nonlinearity to filter out small cross-talk. This directly explains empirically-observed "polysemantic" neurons (a single neuron axis participates in encoding multiple superposed feature directions) without breaking the linear-direction-as-feature picture — the feature is still a direction, just not one that lines up with any single neuron axis.
  - Park, Choe & Veitch (2024) give a later, more formal statement of the LRH: they define linear representations via causal/counterfactual interventions along a direction and connect the geometry (e.g., orthogonality of directions for independent concepts) to the model's own inner-product structure, and extend it to hierarchical/categorical concepts (Park, Choe, Jiang & Veitch, 2024) — this is the modern formalization the empirical steering-vector literature (activation addition, linear probing) leans on.
  - Empirical support: (a) In vision circuits (Olah et al. and follow-on InceptionV1 work), individual/small combinations of feature directions ("curve detectors," high-low frequency detectors) are directly identifiable and causally verified by ablation/optimization (feature visualization). (b) Toy-model superposition experiments (Elhage et al. 2022) reproduce the polysemanticity/superposition phenomenon on synthetic data with known ground-truth features, validating that sparse features are linearly, almost-orthogonally encoded. (c) Steering-vector / activation-addition results across many LLM papers show that adding a single direction (found by e.g. mean-difference of contrastive activations) reliably shifts model behavior along the intended concept axis — an operational confirmation of linearity for many concepts.
  - Pushback: Engels, Liao, Michaud, Gurnee & Tegmark (2024), "Not All Language Model Features Are Linear" (arXiv:2405.14860, originally titled "...One-Dimensionally Linear"), give a rigorous definition of an **irreducible multi-dimensional feature** (one that cannot be decomposed into independent or non-co-occurring 1-D sub-features) and use sparse autoencoders to automatically discover such features in GPT-2 and Mistral-7B — most strikingly **circular representations of days-of-the-week and months-of-the-year**, which the model demonstrably uses to solve modular-arithmetic-style tasks (e.g., "what day is 3 days after Tuesday?"). This is direct counter-evidence to a *strict, one-dimensional-only* reading of the LRH: at least some computationally load-bearing structure is intrinsically multi-dimensional (a 2-D circle, not decomposable into two independent linear features), even though it is still, in a weaker sense, a "linear subspace" phenomenon (the circle lives in a 2-D linear subspace) rather than an arbitrarily nonlinear one.
