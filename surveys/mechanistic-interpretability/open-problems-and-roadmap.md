## <a id="sec-15"></a>15 Open problems & roadmap

<!-- sec:15.1 -->
### <a id="sec-15.1"></a>15.1 Open problems

<a id="p-151-open-problems-1"></a><!-- para:151-open-problems-1 --> Each gap is stated as {what is known · what is unknown · why it matters · state of attack}.

1. <a id="p-151-open-problems-2"></a><!-- para:151-open-problems-2 --> **No robust faithfulness metric.** *Known:* faithfulness/completeness/minimality are the right axes <!-- cite:35 --> [[35]](references.md#ref-35), <!-- cite:40 --> [[40]](references.md#ref-40). *Unknown:* a metric stable to ablation convention, averaging order, and prompt format — the current one swings >50 points <!-- cite:62 --> [[62]](references.md#ref-62). *Why it matters:* every circuit claim rests on it; without a stable metric, "we found the circuit" is not falsifiable. *State of attack:* early — the field has diagnosed the problem <!-- cite:62 --> [[62]](references.md#ref-62) but not fixed it.
2. **SAE "dark matter."** *Known:* SAEs leave a large, *structured*, linearly-predictable residual <!-- cite:16 --> [[16]](references.md#ref-16). *Unknown:* what that missed structure is and whether a different objective captures it. *Why it matters:* it bounds how much of the model an SAE can ever explain. *State of attack:* new objectives (matching pursuit <!-- cite:77 --> [[77]](references.md#ref-77), transcoders <!-- cite:18 --> [[18]](references.md#ref-18)) are partly motivated by this.
3. **Feature geometry beyond one dimension.** *Known:* some features are irreducibly multi-dimensional (day/month circles) <!-- cite:5 --> [[5]](references.md#ref-5). *Unknown:* how common non-linear/multi-dimensional structure is, and whether dictionary learning should target it. *Why it matters:* the LRH is the foundation of probing, steering, and SAEs. *State of attack:* nascent.
4. **Does circuit analysis scale?** *Known:* attribution graphs give frontier-scale circuits for single prompts <!-- cite:20 --> [[20]](references.md#ref-20), <!-- cite:21 --> [[21]](references.md#ref-21). *Unknown:* whether *complete*, *global* circuits (not per-input graphs) are tractable at scale, and whether frozen-attention is safe. *Why it matters:* per-input graphs are not a full mechanistic model. *State of attack:* active; complete-replacement-model follow-ups are appearing.
5. **Self-repair at frontier scale.** *Known:* self-repair is real and dilutes ablation metrics <!-- cite:59 --> [[59]](references.md#ref-59), <!-- cite:60 --> [[60]](references.md#ref-60). *Unknown:* its magnitude in current frontier models (measured mostly at GPT-2/Chinchilla scale). *Why it matters:* it silently biases every faithfulness number upward in magnitude of importance. *State of attack:* open (§ <!-- secxref:9.5 -->[§9.5](circuits-across-models.md#sec-9.5)).
6. **Adversarial robustness of safety probes.** *Known:* probes catch non-adversarial deception at >99% AUROC <!-- cite:71 --> [[71]](references.md#ref-71). *Unknown:* how to make detection robust when a red team optimizes against it (sandbagging) <!-- cite:78 --> [[78]](references.md#ref-78). *Why it matters:* it is the difference between a monitoring demo and a safety guarantee. *State of attack:* early; the current honest answer is "not robust."
7. **Automation faithfulness.** *Known:* auto-interp scales feature labeling cheaply <!-- cite:68 --> [[68]](references.md#ref-68), <!-- cite:69 --> [[69]](references.md#ref-69). *Unknown:* how to guarantee an explanation that *predicts* activations also captures the *causal* role. *Why it matters:* millions of features can only be interpreted automatically. *State of attack:* proxy metrics only.
8. **SAE downstream usefulness.** *Known:* SAEs lose to simple baselines on steering/probing <!-- cite:66 --> [[66]](references.md#ref-66), <!-- cite:67 --> [[67]](references.md#ref-67). *Unknown:* whether a redesigned objective, or a fundamentally different abstraction, recovers a downstream win. *Why it matters:* it decides whether dictionary learning is a discovery tool or a dead end for *action*. *State of attack:* the field's most active debate (§ <!-- secxref:12.2 -->[§12.2](state-of-the-art-and-practice.md#sec-12.2)).

### <a id="sec-15.2"></a>15.2 Reference-implementation handoff

<a id="p-152-reference-implementation-handoff-1"></a><!-- para:152-reference-implementation-handoff-1 --> Three questions are *study-ready* — a clear baseline-to-beat, a closed-form or prior-reported predicted margin, and a falsifiable hypothesis a downstream `reference-implementation-study` could test on open models (Gemma 2 via Gemma Scope <!-- cite:65 --> [[65]](references.md#ref-65), GPT-2 via open SAEs). These are filed in `todos/2026-07-01-mechinterp-ris-handoff.md`.

| Candidate study | Baseline to beat | Predicted margin (hypothesis) | What it would settle |
|---|---|---|---|
| SAE architecture on the fidelity–sparsity frontier (Gemma 2 2B, one site) | ReLU SAE at matched $L_0$ | JumpReLU/TopK Pareto-dominate ReLU; loss-recovered gap grows with width <!-- cite:11 --> [[11]](references.md#ref-11), <!-- cite:12 --> [[12]](references.md#ref-12) | reproduces the shrinkage-fix claim on an open suite |
| Attribution vs. exact patching faithfulness (IOI, Greater-Than) | plain EAP | EAP-IG lifts IOI faithfulness from ~0% toward the exact-patching curve <!-- cite:41 --> [[41]](references.md#ref-41) | quantifies the linearization error and the IG fix |
| Steering: prompting vs. diff-in-means vs. SAE-clamp (Gemma 2 2B/9B) | prompting | prompting $\ge$ diff-in-means $\ge$ naive SAE steering at matched coherence <!-- cite:66 --> [[66]](references.md#ref-66) | replicates the SAE-debate result on a fixed harness |

<a id="p-152-reference-implementation-handoff-2"></a><!-- para:152-reference-implementation-handoff-2 --> Each carries a Quantitative (not merely Directional) hypothesis wherever a prior paper reports a magnitude, so a reproduction study can overlay the analytic/prior-reported prediction on its own measurements — the theory-as-predictor discipline of the report-completeness rule.

<a id="p-152-reference-implementation-handoff-3"></a><!-- para:152-reference-implementation-handoff-3 --> **Roadmap, in one sentence.** The near-term trajectory is: fix the evaluation crisis (a robust faithfulness metric, better SAE downstream benchmarks), consolidate the SAE→transcoder→attribution-graph pivot into a scalable *global* circuit method, and — the field's defining challenge — turn non-adversarial monitoring wins into *adversarially robust* safety tools before the capabilities they would police arrive <!-- cite:76 --> [[76]](references.md#ref-76).
