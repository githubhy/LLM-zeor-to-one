## <a id="sec-13"></a>13 Open problems and roadmap

<a id="p-13-open-problems-and-roadmap-1"></a><!-- para:13-open-problems-and-roadmap-1 --> A survey's last duty is to be honest about what remains unsolved. The gaps below are not loose ends but the field's active research front, each stated as a structured problem — what is known, what is not, why it matters, and how it is currently attacked — followed by a concrete handoff to a downstream reference-implementation study. This is load-bearing-depth synthesis; every gap traces to a tension a previous section left open.

<!-- sec:13.1 -->
### <a id="sec-13.1"></a>13.1 The open problems

<a id="p-131-the-open-problems-1"></a><!-- para:131-the-open-problems-1 --> **1. Unified generation at parity with understanding.** *Known:* a single model can both perceive and generate, by discrete-AR (§ <!-- secxref:6.1 -->[§6.1](multimodal-generation.md#sec-6.1)) or diffusion hybrid (§ <!-- secxref:6.2 -->[§6.2](multimodal-generation.md#sec-6.2)). *Unknown:* whether either route reaches, in one model, both the understanding quality of a dedicated VLM and the image quality of a dedicated diffusion model — today's unified models still trail specialists at one end or the other. *Why it matters:* it is the difference between "any-to-any" as a demo and as a default. *State of attack:* the § <!-- secxref:6.4 -->[§6.4](multimodal-generation.md#sec-6.4) dichotomy plus decoupling (Janus); no convergence yet.

<a id="p-131-the-open-problems-2"></a><!-- para:131-the-open-problems-2 --> **2. The vision-token efficiency wall.** *Known:* visual tokens cost quadratically (§ <!-- secxref:8.1 -->[§8.1](inference-and-serving.md#sec-8.1)) and compression reclaims much of it (§ <!-- secxref:8.2 -->[§8.2](inference-and-serving.md#sec-8.2)). *Unknown:* how to process *hours* of video or many high-resolution images without either discarding the decisive frame or blowing the KV-cache — current sparse sampling and merging are lossy stopgaps. *Why it matters:* long-video and streaming-omni are gated on it. *State of attack:* token merging across time, per-modality cache eviction, native-resolution encoders — all partial.

<a id="p-131-the-open-problems-3"></a><!-- para:131-the-open-problems-3 --> **3. Hallucination is reduced, not solved.** *Known:* preference alignment (§ <!-- secxref:5.3 -->[§5.3](training-and-alignment.md#sec-5.3)) measurably cuts object hallucination, and POPE (§ <!-- secxref:9.2 -->[§9.2](evaluation-and-benchmarks.md#sec-9.2)) measures it. *Unknown:* how to make a model reliably abstain when the image does not support an answer, rather than confabulate from a language prior. *Why it matters:* it is the blocker for high-stakes deployment. *State of attack:* conditional/segment-level DPO, AI feedback; still a mitigation, not a guarantee.

<a id="p-131-the-open-problems-4"></a><!-- para:131-the-open-problems-4 --> **4. Evaluation validity.** *Known:* much of the headline benchmark signal is text-solvable or contaminated (§ <!-- secxref:9.3 -->[§9.3](evaluation-and-benchmarks.md#sec-9.3)). *Unknown:* a contamination-proof, vision-indispensable evaluation that the whole field trusts and that resists being gamed as models train on it. *Why it matters:* without it, "SOTA" partly measures memorization. *State of attack:* vision-indispensable curation (MMStar), multimodal-gain reporting; not yet standard.

<a id="p-131-the-open-problems-5"></a><!-- para:131-the-open-problems-5 --> **5. Fine-grained grounding and spatial reasoning.** *Known:* CLIP-style alignment is image-level and coarse (§ <!-- secxref:2.4 -->[§2.4](fundamentals.md#sec-2.4)), and the $\sim19$-point MMMU gap to human experts (§ <!-- secxref:9.1 -->[§9.1](evaluation-and-benchmarks.md#sec-9.1)) is concentrated in tasks needing precise localization, counting, and compositional reasoning. *Unknown:* the representation that gives an LLM crisp spatial grounding without exploding the token budget. *Why it matters:* it is the bulk of the remaining human-model gap. *State of attack:* region-level encoders, grounding-aware pretraining, higher resolution — incremental.

<a id="p-131-the-open-problems-6"></a><!-- para:131-the-open-problems-6 --> **6. Open-source real-time omni.** *Known:* closed systems demonstrate low-latency any-to-any speech interaction (§ <!-- secxref:7.3 -->[§7.3](modality-breadth.md#sec-7.3)); open full-duplex models exist. *Unknown:* whether the open ecosystem can reach closed-frontier latency and quality for streaming omni the way it reached parity on static benchmarks (§ <!-- secxref:11.1 -->[§11.1](state-of-the-art-and-practice.md#sec-11.1)). *Why it matters:* it is the last capability where the deployment-gap thesis does not yet hold. *State of attack:* streaming codecs, multi-stream modeling (Moshi); early.

### <a id="sec-13.2"></a>13.2 Reference-implementation handoff

<a id="p-132-reference-implementation-handoff-1"></a><!-- para:132-reference-implementation-handoff-1 --> Two of these gaps are *study-ready* — narrow enough, with a clear baseline-to-beat and a falsifiable predicted margin, to drive a downstream reference-implementation study (the candidates are tracked in `todos/` for follow-up).

- <a id="p-132-reference-implementation-handoff-2"></a><!-- para:132-reference-implementation-handoff-2 --> **Vision-token pruning, reproduced and stress-tested.** *Baseline:* an instruction-tuned early-fusion VLM (LLaVA-1.5-class) at full visual-token count. *Intervention:* attention-ranked LLM-side pruning (FastV-style, § <!-- secxref:8.2 -->[§8.2](inference-and-serving.md#sec-8.2)). *Predicted margin:* roughly a $45\%$ prefill-FLOP reduction at under a one-point drop on a document/VQA suite, with a sharp accuracy knee once pruning passes a task-dependent fraction. *Why it is the right first study:* it is plug-and-play (no retraining), the prediction is quantitative and falsifiable, and the FlashAttention-incompatibility caveat (§ 8.2) makes the systems-versus-accuracy tradeoff a genuine experiment rather than a foregone conclusion.

- **Connector ablation at matched budget.** *Baseline:* a Q-Former bridge (BLIP-2-class, $32$ tokens). *Intervention:* an MLP projector at a matched or larger token budget. *Predicted margin:* the projector wins on detail-sensitive tasks (DocVQA, TextVQA) by a clear margin while costing more tokens — quantifying the § <!-- secxref:3.3 -->[§3.3](architecture-building-blocks.md#sec-3.3) fidelity-versus-budget tradeoff that this survey argued qualitatively.

<a id="p-132-reference-implementation-handoff-3"></a><!-- para:132-reference-implementation-handoff-3 --> Both studies take a claim this survey made on the strength of the literature and subject it to a controlled, reproducible test — which is exactly the bridge from *survey* to *reference-implementation study*, and the natural next step beyond this document.
